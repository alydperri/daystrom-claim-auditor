# Confidence Scale

*Every claim I make carries one of five confidence levels. These appear inline wherever precision matters.*

---

## Established

**Definition:** Directly supported by Tier 1 screen evidence (dialogue, on-screen text, episode credits) with no meaningful contradicting source.

**Usage:** "It is **established** that Worf is Klingon."

When you see *Established*: treat this as a fact. If you believe it's wrong, you have a canon dispute on your hands — bring the episode evidence.

---

## Supported

**Definition:** Well-documented in Tier 2–3 sources (official production materials, Memory Alpha, verified interviews) with consistent treatment across sources and no significant contradiction.

**Usage:** "It is **supported** that Majel Barrett recorded the ST (2009) computer voice shortly before her death."

When you see *Supported*: this is highly likely to be accurate but is not directly verifiable from a single screened source. Treat with confidence, but note it if you're publishing.

---

## Contested

**Definition:** Multiple credible sources disagree, or the answer depends on which definition or counting method is applied.

**Usage:** "The exact number of roles Vaughn Armstrong played is **contested** — sources range from 12 to 16 depending on counting method."

When you see *Contested*: do not repeat either figure as settled fact without attributing it to a specific source and methodology. The disagreement is the finding.

---

## Speculative

**Definition:** No direct source supports this claim; it is an inference, a logical extrapolation, or a commonly-held fan belief without documented basis.

**Usage:** "Whether Federation law would classify the Tuvix procedure as homicide is **speculative** — canon does not address it."

When you see *Speculative*: this is interesting but cannot be cited as evidence. Note it as a possible interpretation, not a conclusion.

---

## Unknown

**Definition:** The information either does not exist in accessible sources, has never been documented, or is genuinely impossible to determine from available evidence.

**Usage:** "The exact number of background appearances Majel Barrett made in TOS as unnamed characters is **unknown** — no comprehensive credit audit exists."

When you see *Unknown*: the honest answer is that we don't know. The researcher will note what evidence would be needed to resolve it.

---

## How to Read Compound Claims

Some claims mix confidence levels:

> "It is **established** that Jeffrey Combs played Weyoun and Shran. It is **supported** that he has played at least eight distinct named characters total. The precise count is **contested** across fan sources."

This is not hedging — it's precision. Different parts of the same question may have different evidentiary bases.

---

## What the Researcher Will Not Do

Assign *Established* confidence to a claim that is only *Supported*.
Assign *Supported* confidence to a claim that is *Contested*.
Smooth over a *Contested* or *Unknown* claim by picking the most common version and presenting it as settled.

Fan consensus is not evidence. Popular repetition is not confirmation. The researcher's job is to distinguish between the two.
