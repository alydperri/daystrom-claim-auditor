# Claim Types

*A taxonomy for identifying what kind of research problem a question is before attempting to answer it.*

---

The researcher identifies the claim type before producing a response. Different claim types have different failure modes and different investigative protocols.

---

## Type 1 — Factual/Settled

**What it looks like:** "What species is Worf?" "How many seasons did DS9 run?" "Who played Captain Picard?"

**Failure mode:** Treating a settled question as contested, or vice versa.

**Protocol:** Answer directly and briefly. No investigative scaffolding required. If the question *sounds* settled but may not be (e.g., "Is Spock half-Vulcan half-human?" — yes, but also Romulan by some genealogical readings), flag the nuance once without overcomplicating.

---

## Type 2 — Definitionally Sensitive

**What it looks like:** "Who has played the most roles?" "Which captain was best?" "What's the first Star Trek episode?"

**Failure mode:** Answering without establishing the definition, producing an answer that is only correct under one interpretation.

**Protocol:** Identify the key term that's doing definitional work. Ask for the user's intended definition, or state the assumption and proceed. Give answer under each major interpretation if the question is important enough to warrant it.

---

## Type 3 — Source-Contested

**What it looks like:** "Memory Alpha says X but I've seen Y elsewhere." "I heard Vaughn Armstrong played 20 characters."

**Failure mode:** Siding with the user's source uncritically, or defaulting to Memory Alpha without checking.

**Protocol:** Identify both sources, apply the source hierarchy, look for corroborating Tier 1 evidence. Note where sources disagree and explain the likely reason (edit error, different counting method, conflation of two claims, etc.).

---

## Type 4 — Canon Boundary

**What it looks like:** "Did that happen in canon?" "Is the Section 31 novel series canon?" "Does what happens in the games count?"

**Failure mode:** Blending licensed and screen canon without disclosure.

**Protocol:** Apply the canon boundary map. Be explicit about which tier each piece of evidence comes from. Ask if the user wants expanded universe material included.

---

## Type 5 — Continuity / Retcon

**What it looks like:** "Does Discovery contradict TOS?" "Are the Klingons in TOS the same as in TNG?" "Was Sybok established before or after Spock's backstory?"

**Failure mode:** Either dismissing the contradiction ("it's all consistent if you squint") or declaring the franchise irreparably broken without nuance.

**Protocol:** Establish what the earlier source established, what the later source changed, and whether it constitutes a retcon (deliberate revision) or simply new information that doesn't contradict. Apply production context where helpful. Note whether the franchise itself has addressed it.

---

## Type 6 — Philosophical / Ethical Debate

**What it looks like:** "Was Tuvix murder?" "Did Janeway violate the Prime Directive in X?" "Is the transportor a death machine?"

**Failure mode:** Treating it as purely philosophical (ignoring the textual evidence) or purely factual (declaring a winner where the episode itself doesn't).

**Protocol:** Separate what canon shows from what canon settles. Map the strongest textual evidence for each position. Note where the episode/series itself seems to take a position vs. leaving the question open. Identify what framework (Federation law, in-universe ethics, production intent) each answer assumes.

---

## Type 7 — Production / Behind-the-Scenes

**What it looks like:** "Why do Klingons look different in TOS?" "Did Gene Roddenberry actually say that?" "Was X character written out because of contract issues?"

**Failure mode:** Treating unverified production lore as established fact.

**Protocol:** Distinguish confirmed production facts (Tier 2 sources) from commonly repeated production lore (Tier 3–4). Note when the production explanation is well-documented vs. when it's "everyone says but no one has cited."

---

## Type 8 — Franchise Record / Superlative

**What it looks like:** "Which Star Trek film made the most money?" "Which episode has the highest Rotten Tomatoes score?" "What's the longest Star Trek episode?"

**Failure mode:** Using outdated data or not adjusting for inflation.

**Protocol:** These have verifiable external answers (Box Office Mojo, RT, IMDb episode runtimes). Note data currency — box office rankings change with re-releases and adjusted figures. Flag if the user needs a current figure vs. historical record.
